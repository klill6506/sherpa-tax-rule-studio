## What this is

Form 2848 authorizes an **individual eligible to practice before the IRS** (Part II designations a–r) to
represent the taxpayer and to inspect/receive confidential tax information. Boundaries: **Form 8821** =
inspect/receive only, NO representation; **Form 56** = fiduciary (stands AS the taxpayer, not a
representative); a non-IRS POA is acceptable per Pub. 216 / 26 CFR 601.503(a) but cannot be CAF-recorded
unless a completed 2848 is attached (taxpayer signature not required on that 2848; representative must
still sign Part II). A separate Form 2848 is required **per taxpayer** — joint filers each file their own.
2848 does NOT change the last-known address (that's 8822/8822-B). Structural/administrative — no tax
computation; print-first (mail/fax/online-upload; no MeF).

## The form face (verbatim map, Rev. 1-2021 — 2 pages)

**Part I — Power of Attorney**
- **L1** Taxpayer information: name + address · TIN(s) · daytime phone · plan number (if applicable).
- **L2** Representatives (FOUR blocks): name + address · **CAF No.** · **PTIN** · phone · fax ·
  "Check if new: Address / Telephone No. / Fax No." · blocks 1-2 carry "Check if to be sent copies of
  notices and communications"; blocks 3-4 instead carry the note **"IRS sends notices and communications
  to only two representatives."**
- **L3** Acts authorized (REQUIRED) — three-column grid: **Description of Matter** (Income, Employment,
  Payroll, Excise, Estate, Gift, Whistleblower, Practitioner Discipline, PLR, FOIA, Civil Penalty,
  Sec. 4980H Shared Responsibility Payment, etc.) · **Tax Form Number** · **Year(s) or Period(s)**.
- **L4** Specific use not recorded on CAF (checkbox).
- **L5a** Additional acts authorized — checkboxes: Access my IRS records via an Intermediate Service
  Provider · Authorize disclosure to third parties · Substitute or add representative(s) · Sign a return ·
  Other acts authorized (text).
- **L5b** Specific acts not authorized — the built-in check-negotiation prohibition (representatives may
  never endorse/negotiate government checks or direct payment into their own accounts) + free-text
  deletions.
- **L6** Retention/revocation: filing **automatically revokes all earlier POAs on file for the same
  matters and years/periods** — check the box to NOT revoke, and **"YOU MUST ATTACH A COPY OF ANY POWER OF
  ATTORNEY YOU WANT TO REMAIN IN EFFECT."**
- **L7** Taxpayer declaration and signature (authority certification for corporate officer / partner /
  guardian / TMP / PR-or-DI / executor / receiver / administrator / trustee signers) — **"IF NOT
  COMPLETED, SIGNED, AND DATED, THE IRS WILL RETURN THIS POWER OF ATTORNEY TO THE TAXPAYER."**

**Part II — Declaration of Representative** (perjury declaration: not suspended/disbarred; subject to
Circular 230; authorized to represent): designation letters **a** Attorney · **b** CPA · **c** Enrolled
Agent · **d** Officer · **e** Full-Time Employee · **f** Family Member (spouse, parent, child,
grandparent, grandchild, step-parent, step-child, brother, or sister) · **g** Enrolled Actuary (Circular
230 §10.3(d) limits) · **h** Unenrolled Return Preparer (4 conditions incl. AFSP) · **k** Qualifying
Student or Law Graduate (LITC/STCP) · **r** Enrolled Retirement Plan Agent (§10.3(e) limits). Columns:
designation letter · licensing jurisdiction · bar/license/certification/registration/enrollment number ·
signature · date. **Representatives must sign in the order listed in line 2**; d–f enter
title/position/relationship in the jurisdiction column. Unsigned Part II → the IRS returns the POA.

## Key rules (verbatim substance)

- **L3 validity:** description of matter + tax form number (where applicable) + year(s)/period(s) (where
  applicable) are REQUIRED. Ranges via "through," "thru," or a hyphen ("2018 thru 2020", "2nd 2018–3rd
  2019"); fiscal years in **YYYYMM**; short periods as begin–end dates. **"Do not use a general reference
  such as 'All years,' 'All periods,' or 'All taxes.' The IRS will return any power of attorney with a
  general reference."** Estate tax → decedent's date of death instead of a period. 1065/1120-S matters =
  "Income including pass-through items"; BBA matters = "Centralized Partnership Audit Regime (BBA)".
  Return-related penalties/interest ride the line-3 authorization by default (unless 5b); non-return
  penalties need "Civil Penalty" (IRA penalties: "IRA Civil Penalty") with years and "Not Applicable" in
  the form column.
- **Future periods:** current + ended periods always listable; future periods OK **but the CAF will not
  record future years/periods beyond 3 years from December 31 of the year the IRS receives the POA**
  (received 2026 → recordable through 2029).
- **Representatives:** max FOUR on one form ("See attached for additional representatives" + extra 2848s
  for more); **no more than TWO** may receive notice copies; CAF number is the 9-digit rep identifier
  (enter "None" for a first-timer — the IRS issues one); forgotten CAF numbers via PPS 866-860-4259;
  post-employment restrictions (18 U.S.C. 207 / Circular 230 §10.25) for former federal employees; LITC/
  STCP students list the lead attorney/CPA FIRST on line 2.
- **Signature mechanics (L7 + Part II):** **handwritten signatures required for mail/fax filings —
  digital/electronic/typed-font signatures are INVALID on mailed/faxed forms**; electronic signatures are
  accepted ONLY via online submission (IRS.gov/Submit2848), with mandatory identity authentication for
  remote transactions (photo-ID compare + data recording + secondary-document verification, individual and
  entity variants). **Sequence rule: if the taxpayer signs first, the representative must sign within 45
  days (60 days for taxpayers residing abroad); if the representative signs first, no time limit applies
  to the taxpayer.** Joint filers → separate 2848s. Corporations: a binding officer + exact title;
  partnerships: all partners, or the one with state-law binding authority (+ attach the authorization);
  BBA: the PR (title "Partnership Representative") or DI ("Designated Individual of [PR name]"); estates:
  one binding co-executor (26 CFR 601.503(d)).
- **Authority granted / L5a:** the POA covers all acts the taxpayer can perform for the listed matters
  EXCEPT check negotiation (always barred), and does NOT include ISP record access, third-party
  disclosure, substituting/adding representatives, or signing a return unless the 5a boxes grant them.
  Sign-a-return rides **§1.6012-1(a)(5)** only: (a) disease or injury, (b) continuous absence from the US
  ≥60 days before the filing deadline, (c) IRS permission for other good cause — with the prescribed
  statement on the 5a lines (agent-not-representative variant adds line 4 + the "No other acts…" sentence
  + skips Part II). Form 907 signing needs explicit 5a language.
- **⚠ NEW (Rec. Dev. 08-Jul-2026) — "modified" CAF status:** a 2848 records as **"modified"** when line
  5a contains authorizations other than disclosure / substitution / return-signing, or when **line 5b
  limits the representative's authority in any way** — a modified POA **blocks Transcript Delivery System
  access and Tax Pro Account installment agreements** for the representative. And: **"Taxpayers and
  representatives should never check line 4 unless Form 2848 is, in fact, a specific-use form"** (line 4
  prevents CAF recording entirely).
- **Unenrolled return preparers (designation h):** may represent ONLY before revenue agents, customer
  service representatives, or similar IRS officers/employees (incl. TAS) **during an examination of the
  period covered by the return they prepared and signed**; never before appeals officers, revenue
  officers, or Chief Counsel; cannot execute closing agreements, extend assessment/collection statutes,
  execute waivers or refund claims, or sign any document. Requirements: valid active PTIN + eligibility to
  sign the return under exam + (post-2015 returns) a valid **AFSP Record of Completion for BOTH the
  preparation year AND the representation year(s)**. Falls back to Form 8821 (info-only) if unmet.
- **L4 specific-use list (not CAF-recorded):** PLR/technical advice · EIN applications · Form 843 claims ·
  corporate dissolutions · Circular 230 proceedings · **accounting method/period changes** (the 3115/
  WO-23 adjacency) · exemption applications (1023/1024/1028) · plan determinations (5300/5307/5316/5310) ·
  W-7 ITIN · Form 4361 · §7623 whistleblower awards · EPCRS · FOIA. Specific-use POAs go to the office
  handling the matter and revoke only same-office/same-matter priors.
- **Revocation/withdrawal:** taxpayer writes **"REVOKE"** (representative: **"WITHDRAW"**) across the top
  of page 1 with current signature + date, then mail/fax per the chart; the no-copy alternative is a
  signed statement listing matters/periods (or "revoke all years/periods" — allowed THERE, unlike line 3)
  and each representative. Filing a 2848 never revokes a Form 8821. A newly designated BBA PR must file a
  NEW 2848 (do not check line 6).
- **Students/law graduates (k):** TAS authorization letter attached; may fully represent per the letter;
  **the CAF auto-purges a student representative 130 days after the taxpayer's signature date**. Any
  individual may represent outside the United States (Circular 230 §10.7(c)(1)(vii)) — jurisdiction column
  blank.
